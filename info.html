Born: February 20, 1991 – Castlebar, Ireland
Occupation: Novelist, Screenwriter
Education: Trinity College Dublin (BA, MPhil)

Sally Rooney grew up in the west of Ireland. She studied English literature and later earned a master's degree in American literature.

Her debut novel gained critical acclaim, and by her late twenties, she was dubbed "the first great millennial author.

"

She is known for her quiet, introspective style and her exploration of communication, emotional intimacy, and class dynamics in modern life.

Rooney's writing often focuses on the internal lives of her characters, depicting the complexity of human relationships with striking realism.

Rooney has been compared to authors like Jane Austen and J.D. Salinger, but her voice is distinctly modern, capturing the psychological landscape of young people navigating love, power, and politics. Her work is celebrated for its emotional intelligence, sparse prose, and cultural relevance.

Despite her fame, Rooney is known for her privacy and thoughtful critique of fame and capitalism—topics that she sometimes weaves into her fiction. She has also voiced political views, especially regarding social justice and the role of art in society.

In 2024, she released a new short story titled Intermezzo, further fueling speculation about a possible fourth novel. Readers and critics alike continue to follow her work with great anticipation.
